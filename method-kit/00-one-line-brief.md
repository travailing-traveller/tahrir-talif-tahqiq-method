# Step 0 — The One-Line Brief

*The first move of any Taḥrīr / Taʾlīf / Taḥqīq project, before discovery begins.*

Write a **single line** describing the project you intend to produce. One line.
It forces you to know what you are making before the method helps you make it,
and it becomes the spine of the Discovery Gate that follows.

A good one-line brief names three things:

- **What** — the artefact (a verified digest, a critical apparatus, a translated
  selection, a referenced sermon series, a lexical study…).
- **From what** — the source(s) and edition(s) it rests on.
- **For whom / in what register** — the audience and the primary form.

Shape (illustrative, not to copy):

> "A verified, referenced English digest of [work], from [edition], for
> [audience], delivered as [primary register]."

Then begin the Discovery Gate (`01-discovery-gate.md`). If you cannot yet write
the line, that is itself the first thing the Gate will help you resolve — but you
may not skip past it.
