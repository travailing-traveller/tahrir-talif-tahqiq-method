# Feedback Wanted

This is a draft method shared with a select circle. The most useful contribution is not agreement but precise disagreement, pressure-testing, and counter-examples from real Islamic studies work.

Please mark up the files directly, or note the file, section, and objection.

## 1. The three movements

Is **Taḥrīr / Taʾlīf / Taḥqīq** the right division?

- Does the naming help or mislead?
- Does the order imply wrongly that verification happens only at the end?
- Are any major scholarly operations missing?

## 2. Retrieval-grounding

The method says primary text, vocalisation, attribution, and citations should not be originated from model memory.

- Is this workable in your real projects?
- Where does the rule become too rigid?
- What principled exceptions are needed?
- How should oral teaching notes, remembered references, or uncatalogued sources be handled?

## 3. The unit

The method works one unit at a time.

- Is the unit discipline practical?
- What is the right grain for tafsīr, hadith, fiqh, uṣūl, kalām, taṣawwuf, Arabic grammar, manuscript work, teaching material, or sermon preparation?
- When should units be coarser or finer?

## 4. The Discovery Gate

The method refuses to proceed on inadequate input.

- Is this too rigid, or exactly right?
- Where is the line between rigour and obstruction?
- Which questions should be mandatory?
- Which questions should be optional by project type?

## 5. Verification

The method distinguishes machine-checkable verification from human-only judgement.

- What does the deterministic harness miss?
- Which Arabic normalisation choices are debatable?
- How should tashkīl, hamza, alif forms, tāʾ marbūṭa, alif maqṣūra, punctuation, and orthographic variation be handled?
- What should always require human review?

## 6. Registers

The method says registers may compress, reorder, simplify, explain, and apply, but must not smuggle new source-claims.

- Is this workable for sermons and teaching?
- How should faithful application differ from added claims?
- What level of reflective expansion should be allowed?

## 7. Genre-specific risks

What failure modes are missing in:

- tafsīr;
- hadith commentary;
- takhrīj;
- fiqh;
- uṣūl al-fiqh;
- kalām;
- taṣawwuf;
- Arabic grammar and rhetoric;
- manuscript collation;
- translation;
- teaching material;
- sermons and exhortations;
- public-facing summaries?

## 8. Ledgers and files

Are the proposed ledgers useful or too heavy?

- `SOURCE_LEDGER.md`
- `CLAIMS_LEDGER.md`
- `UNITS.md`
- `TERMINOLOGY.md`
- `STYLE_GUIDE.md`
- `QUESTIONS.md`
- `RISKS.md`

Which are essential? Which should be optional?

## 9. Licensing and sharing

The kit is presently licensed under CC BY-NC-SA 4.0.

- Is this suitable for semi-public scholarly feedback?
- Would it discourage useful adoption?
- Should any parts be separated under a different arrangement later?

## 10. Concrete counter-examples

Please provide examples where the method would fail, become cumbersome, or need adaptation.

Concrete examples from real work are more valuable than general approval.
